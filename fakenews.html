Learn the steps to identify fake news!

when you read a news article, follow the steps to identify what is real and what is fake:

STEP 1:

Reflect On Your Reaction

A lot of fake news is designed to get clicks and engagement, it therefore needs to elicit a strong reaction such as outrage, anger, fear or even happiness. So, when you read a news article, if you have a strong reaction to it, make sure to stop and reflect on your reaction. It could be a clue that what you're reading is actually fake news.

STEP 2:

Develop their critical thinking skills

Critical thinking and digital literacy go hand in hand, work on your critical thinking skills by following the steps shown when you come across a news you might think is fake:

First: Think about who created this content?
Second: Think about what else do you know about this topic?
Third: Do some research!

STEP 3:

Look for trustworthy news sources

trustworthy news sources such as Channel News Asia and The Straits Times to confirm if the news is fake or real

STEP 4:

Make sure to further read resources

look for different resources before confirming if the article is real or fake
